Granular Activated Carbon (GAC)

Granular Activated Carbon (GAC) is primarily produced from coconut shells and other carbon-rich organic materials. The activation process involves heating raw materials at temperatures above 950Â°C in a controlled, oxygen-free environment. The material is then exposed to steam or inert gases such as nitrogen or argon to develop its porous structure. After activation, the carbon is screened and graded to obtain the desired granule size. GAC is widely used for water purification, air treatment, odor control, and industrial filtration systems due to its durability and high adsorption capacity.

Powdered Activated Carbon (PAC)

Powdered Activated Carbon is engineered to have a vast number of microscopic pores, enabling superior adsorption efficiency. PAC is created by finely grinding activated carbon and passing it through precision filters to achieve uniform particle sizes. Due to its rapid adsorption kinetics, PAC is ideal for applications where quick purification is required, including wastewater treatment, food and beverage processing, and chemical purification. Its high surface area and diffusion rate give it a significant advantage over conventional filtration media.
