How Activated Carbon Works in Water Treatment

By Laxmi International

Activated carbon is one of the most effective filtration media used in modern water treatment systems. Its performance is rooted in adsorption, a process where contaminants adhere to its internal porous surface. These pores, formed through high-temperature activation, create a massive surface area capable of removing chlorine, tastes, odours, organic chemicals, and industrial impurities. This is why activated carbon is a crucial element in municipal, commercial, and industrial water purification.

In water treatment plants, granular activated carbon (GAC) is typically used in fixed-bed filters due to its ability to withstand continuous flow. Its pore structure captures dissolved organic matter and chemical residues, improving water clarity and safety. Laxmi International supplies GAC grades engineered specifically for long bed life, uniform particle size, and reduced pressure drop, ensuring optimal filtration performance in a variety of plant configurations.

Powdered activated carbon (PAC) is used when rapid treatment is needed. It is added directly to the water stream and acts quickly, making it ideal for seasonal contamination, emergency treatment, or taste-and-odor issues. Laxmi International offers PAC with high surface area and fast adsorption kinetics, helping facilities respond effectively to sudden changes in raw water quality.

Whether used in municipal drinking water systems, wastewater treatment plants, or industrial filtration units, activated carbon remains essential for safeguarding public health. Through its high adsorption capacity and proven reliability, it continues to be the backbone of effective water purification.
