Importance of Water Treatment

The whole purpose of water treatment is to make sure that all kinds of impurities are extracted from water so that it can be made safe for regular human consumption. In circumstances where pure and clean water is rare, it is very important to make use of water treatment as this can help people of all ages to drink clean water and stay healthy. As pollution is a major problem in the modern world, it can be difficult for people to get clean water readily that they can use for drinking. Therefore it is vitally important to make use of activated carbon in such cases as it can help in purifying water. The unique properties of activated carbon can help in filtrating water and it is even more effective than traditional methods of filtration. Activated carbon is now regarded as one of the most pocket friendly ways to remove contaminants from the water. It can also help to eradicate bad smell that may be present in the drinking water.

The Functions of Activated Carbon

The main reason for which activated carbon is used to treat water is that activated charcoal can serve as a powerful adsorbent, thus getting rid of unwanted organics and particles from unclean water. The process of removing these particles is achieved when they get bound to the activated carbon particles through physical or chemical attraction. In many ways, this is the best method of removing germs and undesired particles from the water as otherwise they can react with disinfectants like chlorine and give rise to new byproducts. Such byproducts can also lead to cancer or maybe even fatal when they are consumed. Therefore it can be said that activated charcoal can be a great way to maintain human health at a very affordable price.

Adsorption Process Associated With Water Treatment

The distinct feature of adsorption is what makes activated carbon perfect for water treatment. For this reason, activated carbon is used in homes as well as large factories for water purification. The process of purification is carried out in three different steps. During the first step, all unwanted particles get adsorbed to the outer surface of carbon granules. The particles then move to the carbon pores. The final step involves complete adsorption of particles to interior walls of activated charcoal or carbon. This gives rise to clean, drinkable water. Using activated carbon for water purification is also very simple and it is for this reason that this process is widely accepted.
