Granular vs Powdered Activated Carbon — Which for Filtration?

By laxmi-international-admin

Granular Activated Carbon (GAC) and Powdered Activated Carbon (PAC) serve distinct roles in filtration. GAC is formed into larger particles and placed in fixed-bed filters, providing long contact time and easy regeneration options.

PAC is finely ground, dosed directly into liquids for rapid adsorption and is typically removed by clarification or filtration after treatment.

Regeneration & Reuse of Activated Carbon — Methods & Benefits

By laxmi-international-admin

Regenerating spent activated carbon restores adsorption capacity and reduces lifecycle costs and waste. Thermal reactivation is the most common industrial method: spent carbon is heated in controlled oxygen-free conditions to desorb adsorbed organics and restore porosity. Chemical and biological reactivation methods exist but are more specialized and applied to specific contaminant types.
